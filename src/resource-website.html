Resource Website

It is a website dedicated for or Parents of Childhood Cancer Survivors (Children’s Cancer Foundation)

With the high survival rate of childhood cancer survivors (CCS) in Singapore, there's significance placed on the caregiver role, and these caregivers often feel uncertain of their responsibilities and are fearful of a relapse. Studies found that well-informed caregivers coped better with caregiver stress and had reduced anxiety. However, Singapore has no official platform for caregivers to search for survivorship information. I decided to develop a resource website that provides survivorship information and resources for caregiver-parents of CCS.

The website aims to provide caregiver-parents of CCS with relevant and comprehensible survivorship information and to inform readers about Children's Cancer Foundation’s (CCF) survivorship services. These two objectives were achieved by conducting an evaluation survey among staff. 100% of the surveyed staff indicated that they would recommend the website to caregiver-parent clients, meeting the first objective of the website. For the second objective, 85.7% of the surveyed staff agreed that the website would increase caregiver-parents’ awareness of CCF’s services.

The website consists of five main survivorship topics. I interviewed CCF social workers to find out about the needs of caregivers as they enter into survivorship. Online journal articles and websites were also used to learn more about childhood cancer survivors. I learnt that caregivers are always in a constant state of cautiousness for a potential relapse which makes social groups an important factor in supporting the needs of caregivers. As such, the resource website that I created may help support them.
